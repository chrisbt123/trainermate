Keeping your data safe

TrainerMate is built to be a helpful local desktop assistant, not a big online system collecting lots of information. The aim is simple: keep your course admin easier while keeping your data under your control.

Most things stay on your computerYour course list, provider information and certificates are designed to live on your own computer. TrainerMate only sends information out when it needs to, such as checking your licence, using Zoom, or helping with a support request.
Passwords are protectedTrainerMate should not leave passwords sitting around in ordinary text files. Where possible, login details are kept using your computer’s built-in secure password storage.
Zoom works through sign-in permissionYou connect TrainerMate to Zoom by signing in with Zoom and giving permission. TrainerMate should not ask for, see, or store your Zoom password.
Support is kept practicalIf support information is sent, it should be limited to what is needed to diagnose the issue. It should avoid passwords, Zoom tokens, licence keys and unnecessary personal details.
The app is not meant to be publicTrainerMate is intended to run locally on your own machine. Its dashboard is designed for your computer, not for random people on the internet to access.
Safer handling of messages and textAny names, notes or messages shown in the app should be treated as plain text. This helps stop pasted-in content from behaving like website code.
Updates should be checkedApp updates should come from a secure download link and be checked before they are opened, so users are not asked to run something unexpected or unverified.
A sensible reminder: TrainerMate is there to reduce admin time, not replace professional judgement. Trainers should still check course details, Zoom links and provider portal information before relying on them.
