Security, in plain English

TrainerMate handles course admin, Zoom links and certificates, so it is designed to be practical, careful and easy to understand.

What TrainerMate is designed to do safely

Zoom accessTrainerMate uses Zoom sign-in permission. You do not give TrainerMate your Zoom password.
FOBS updatesWhen supported, TrainerMate can create a Zoom meeting and add the joining information to the FOBS meeting details page. It checks first so valid existing details are not replaced unnecessarily.
Certificate filesCertificate tools are designed to help you manage, upload and track provider certificates without sending unnecessary information to support.
Local appTrainerMate runs on your Windows computer. Provider access details, local course data and certificate files are kept local wherever the app can sensibly do that.
Plain alertsThe app tries to explain problems in normal language, such as missing Zoom details, sync due or certificate expiring soon.
Official updatesInstall TrainerMate updates only from official TrainerMate links or prompts inside the app.

Your role

TrainerMate saves time, but trainers should still check the final course details before delivery. This includes confirming the provider record, Zoom joining information and any certificate or compliance requirements.

What not to send by email

Please do not send provider passwords, Zoom passwords, full delegate lists, card details or screenshots with unnecessary personal data. If support needs a screenshot, hide anything that is not relevant.

Independent tool

TrainerMate is independent and is not affiliated with NDORS, UK police forces, course providers, FOBS portal operators or Zoom.

Simple rule: use TrainerMate to reduce repeated admin, but keep the same professional checks you would normally make before delivering a course.
